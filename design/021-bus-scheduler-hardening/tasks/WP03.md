## Objectives & Success Criteria

- `TriggerProtocol` in `src/hassette/types/types.py` has two methods: `first_run_time(current_time)` and `next_run_time(previous_run, current_time)`
- `IntervalTrigger` is stateless ‚Äî `next_run_time()` does not mutate `self`. Uses O(1) integer division for catch-up with a floating-point guard
- `IntervalTrigger.__init__` rejects zero/negative intervals with `ValueError`
- `CronTrigger` creates a fresh `croniter` per call ‚Äî no persistent `self.cron_iter` attribute used by `next_run_time` or `first_run_time`
- `CronTrigger._next_after` pre-checks total gap before entering loop (bounded to ~60 iterations)
- `ScheduledJob.set_next_run()` uses rounded nanos for `sort_index` (consistency fix)
- `scheduler_service.py:283` assertion replaced with warning + 1-second advance
- Caller at `scheduler_service.py:279` passes `(job.next_run, now())`
- Caller at `scheduler.py:519` uses `trigger.first_run_time(now())`
- All existing tests pass; trigger tests no longer mock `now()`
